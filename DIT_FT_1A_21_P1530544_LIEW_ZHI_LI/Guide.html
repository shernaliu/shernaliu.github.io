1. Less is always More


Piling a bunch of accessorise on you body with no focus on your definite style is a no-no. Our fashion advisor, Ms Lynn Koh advises to choose a 1 or 2 accesories that accentuate your outfit the best or emphasise a feature you would want.


What we're saying is wearing a full set of jewlery (earrings, necklaces, bracelets and rings) all at once is too much. Try adorning either a necklace or just earrings and it would look nicer.


2. Don't be so matchy


Sure, having things that matches each other and being equally balance is a good thing, right?


It might not be the case all the time! For example, wearing blue earrings, blue necklace, blue outfit and blue shoes. Not only is there no variation and contrast, it might look out-of-fashion and child-like.


We recommend using the color wheel to find complementary colours. For example, for black coloured outfits, try to pair it with something bright, for example gold coloured necklace.


Take a step back and look at the mirror and use your judgement to see if it looks good on your outfit.


3. Play up to your Features


Accessories can help to highlight a desirable feature on your face and body.


For example:

A long linear earring can elongate your face shape.

A rounded design earring can make your face look rounder and cuter.

Asymmetric earrings can look fun and interesting too!
